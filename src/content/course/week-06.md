# Week 6: The Products – Selling Puts & Calls

You understand the math of Theta and Delta. You know we are the Casino, not the Gambler. Now it is time to learn the two specific transactions we use to turn that math into real income.

These are the Sniper's two weapons: the **Cash-Secured Put** and the **Covered Call**. Used together, they form *The Wheel* — a self-sustaining income system that generates premium in both flat and trending markets.

---

## Weapon 1: The Cash-Secured Put (Bullish Income)

This is your primary weapon when the chart is bullish — Green Ribbon, TFC aligned, Inside Bar pointing up.

**The Setup:** You have cash in your account. You want to own a quality stock, but only at a price you consider cheap.

**The Trade:** "I promise to buy 100 shares of Apple at $140. Pay me $200 for this promise."

You collect $200 upfront. Now two things can happen:

**Scenario A — Stock stays flat or rises:**
The put expires worthless. You keep 100% of the $200 premium. The buyer's "crash insurance" was never needed. You collect and repeat next week.

**Scenario B — Stock falls to your strike:**
You are "assigned" — forced to buy 100 shares at $140. But since you collected $200 in premium, your effective cost basis is $138 per share. You got a quality stock at a discount. If you were truly bullish on it, this was your plan B all along.

> **Cash-Secured Means Cash-Secured:** You must have the cash to buy the shares if assigned. If selling a $140 Put on Apple, you need $14,000 available. This is why we learn vertical spreads in Week 8 — to reduce capital requirements significantly.

---

## Weapon 2: The Covered Call (Income on Existing Stock)

The Covered Call is used when you *already own* 100 shares of a stock and want to collect rent on them — especially when the chart looks flat or slightly bearish short-term but you believe in the stock long-term.

**The Setup:** You own 100 shares of Apple at $150. You want to collect income while holding.

**The Trade:** "I promise to sell my 100 shares of Apple if it hits $165. Pay me $150 for this promise."

**Scenario A — Stock stays flat or drops:**
The call expires worthless. You keep your shares and the $150 premium. You repeat this process next week. This is "collecting rent" on shares you would hold anyway.

**Scenario B — Stock rallies past $165:**
Your shares get "called away" at $165. You keep the $150 premium plus all the stock appreciation from $150 to $165. You miss the move above $165 — but you still made maximum profit for the trade as structured.

---

## The Wheel Strategy — The Full Ecosystem

The Wheel combines both weapons into a self-sustaining income loop:

1. **Sell Puts** on a quality, bullish stock. Collect premium each cycle until assigned.
2. **Get Assigned** — you now own 100 shares at your strike price (your discounted entry).
3. **Sell Covered Calls** above your cost basis. Collect rent while waiting for stock recovery.
4. **Stock gets called away** — sold at a profit above your cost basis.
5. **Back to cash.** Return to Step 1 and repeat.

Each step of the wheel generates premium income. The machine runs perpetually.

> **The Business Model in One Sentence:** Sell puts to get paid while waiting to buy. Sell calls to get paid while waiting to sell. Collect income at every stage of ownership.
