# Week 3: The Signal – Reversals & Continuations

You know how to spot energy compression (Week 1) and you know the direction of the dominant trend (Week 2). Now we need the specific trigger that tells you *exactly when* to enter a trade.

In #TheStrat, there are two families of signals: **Continuations** — trading with an established trend — and **Reversals** — catching the moment a trend changes direction. Both have their place. Both require discipline.

---

## Signal Type 1: The Continuation (2-1-2)

This is the safer of the two signals. You are not predicting a top or bottom — you are joining a trend that is already moving in your favor, after a brief pause.

**Why Continuations Work**

The inside bar "shakes out" weak-handed traders who fear the trend is over. Once the weak hands exit, the original trend resumes with institutional-grade momentum behind it. You enter as those weak hands are being proven wrong.

### The Bullish 2-1-2 Pattern
1. A strong directional up bar (2U) forms — the trend is moving
2. An inside bar (1) forms — price compresses, weak longs exit
3. Price breaks above the inside bar's high — your entry trigger fires

### The Bearish 2-1-2 Pattern
1. A strong directional down bar (2D) forms — downtrend in motion
2. An inside bar (1) forms — bears rest, weak shorts cover
3. Price breaks below the inside bar's low — your short entry fires

---

## Signal Type 2: The Reversal (2-2)

The 2-2 reversal is how we catch a change in trend direction. Most retail traders try to "pick tops and bottoms" by guessing — and get destroyed by premature entries. The 2-2 doesn't require guessing. It requires *confirmation*.

The market must show you the reversal is happening by printing a second directional bar in the opposite direction — and that second bar must then break the high (or low) of the first.

### The Bullish 2-2 Reversal (Catching a Bottom)
1. **Bar A:** A red directional bar (2D) prints — the selloff.
2. **Bar B:** A green directional bar (2U) forms — potential reversal begins.
3. **Trigger:** Bar B breaks *above the HIGH of Bar A*. That is your long entry.

### The Bearish 2-2 Reversal (Catching a Top)
1. **Bar A:** A green directional bar (2U) prints — the rally.
2. **Bar B:** A red directional bar (2D) forms — potential top forming.
3. **Trigger:** Bar B breaks *below the LOW of Bar A*. That is your short entry.

> The 2-2 requires confirmation, not prediction. Everything before the trigger fires is noise — you wait, you watch, you act only when the market confirms the reversal is real.

---

## Continuation vs. Reversal: The Tradeoff

| Attribute | 2-1-2 Continuation | 2-2 Reversal |
|---|---|---|
| Risk level | Lower | Higher |
| TFC requirement | Trend aligned = sufficient | Strong multi-TF confirmation needed |
| Entry trigger | Break of inside bar high/low | Break of Bar A's high/low |
| Recommended for beginners? | Yes — start here | After mastering 2-1-2 |
| Potential upside | Mid-trend move | Full new-trend move |

Start with 2-1-2 continuations until they are automatic. The reversal signals will make more sense once you have the continuation wired in.

---

## The Full Alignment Checklist

Before pulling the trigger on *any* signal, run through this checklist. Every item must be checked. One missing item = no trade.

1. **Monthly:** Is the ribbon green (for longs) or red (for shorts)? Grey/twisting = stop here.
2. **Weekly:** Does it agree with Monthly? Confirmation needed.
3. **Daily:** What bar type is today's candle? Context for the session.
4. **Signal:** Do you have a clear 2-1-2 or 2-2 pattern on your entry timeframe?
5. **Trigger:** Are you entering *at* the exact trigger level — not before, not after chasing?

> **If the Monthly is Red and you are trying to buy — you are the liquidity.** Full alignment protects you from being the exit for smarter money. Respect the checklist every single time.

---

## Summary

The market gives you two types of signals:

* **2-1-2 Continuation** — Join the trend after compression. Lower risk. Higher win rate. Start here.
* **2-2 Reversal** — Catch the turn with confirmation. More upside. Requires experience. Master after 2-1-2.

Both require the Full Alignment Checklist. Both require patience. Neither requires prediction.
