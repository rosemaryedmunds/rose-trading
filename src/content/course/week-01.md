# Week 1: The Engine – Compressing Price

Trading is not about guessing where the price will go. It is about identifying where the **energy is building up**.

Imagine you are holding a spring. If you just hold it loosely, it has no power. But if you place it on a table and push down on it with all your weight, that spring is now dangerous. It isn't moving, but it is full of **Potential Energy**.

The stock market works exactly the same way.

---

## What is #TheStrat?

#TheStrat is a price action framework created by Rob Smith, a professional trader with decades of market experience. The core insight is deceptively simple: **every single candlestick bar in history is one of only three types**, based solely on its relationship to the previous bar's high and low.

No indicators. No oscillators. No lagging averages. Just the raw mathematical structure of how price moves relative to where it just was.

> **Rob Smith's Core Principle:** "All timeframes are in play at all times. The market is always in one of three states: inside, directional, or outside."

What makes #TheStrat powerful is that it gives you a *universal language* for price. Once you internalize it, you see the same structures on a 1-minute SPX chart that appear on a monthly Bitcoin chart. The market is fractal — and TheStrat makes the fractal legible.

## The Three Bar Types

Every bar you will ever see on a chart falls into exactly one of these categories.

### Bar 1 — The Inside Bar (The Spring)

The most powerful signal in trading is the **Inside Bar**.

* **Definition:** A candle that is completely contained within the previous candle's High and Low.
* **The Psychology:** The market is "taking a breath." Buyers and Sellers are in equilibrium. No one is winning. The price is compressing.
* **The Result:** When price finally breaks out of this range, it usually moves violently.
* **TheStrat Number:** 1 — the pause, the compression.

### Bar 2 — The Directional Bar

* **Definition:** Price breaks either the previous high *or* the previous low — but not both.
* **Bullish 2U:** Breaks the previous high. A clear directional statement upward.
* **Bearish 2D:** Breaks the previous low. A clear directional statement downward.
* **The Psychology:** One side has won the auction. Buyers or sellers are in control.

### Bar 3 — The Outside Bar

* **Definition:** Price breaks *both* the previous high and the previous low in the same bar.
* **The Psychology:** Maximum uncertainty. The market absorbed both buyers and sellers.
* **Rule:** Do not enter on a Bar 3. Wait for the next bar to show resolution. The follow-through bar tells the real story.

## The Setup: The "2-1-2"

This is your bread and butter trade. It works on every timeframe and every instrument because it is rooted in human psychology and order flow mechanics.

1. **Event 1 — The 2:** A strong directional move happens (e.g., a big green 2U candle). The trend is in motion.
2. **Event 2 — The 1:** The market pauses. An Inside Bar forms. Weak hands are shaken out. Price compresses inside the prior candle's range.
3. **Event 3 — The Break:** Price breaks above the Inside Bar's high (for longs) or below its low (for shorts).
4. **The Trigger:** You enter the trade exactly when price crosses that level — not before.

> **Key Rule:** We do not predict the breakout. We wait for the price to cross the line. If it doesn't cross, we don't trade. Discipline lives in this rule.

## Bullish and Bearish Combos

TheStrat patterns come in both directions. The most common setups to know:

**Bullish Combos (Long Entries)**
* **2-1-2↑** — Directional up, inside bar, then break above inside bar's high. The classic.
* **1-2-2↑** — Two consecutive directional up bars after compression. Strong momentum continuation.
* **3-1-2↑** — Outside bar chaos, then compression, then resolution upward.

**Bearish Combos (Short Entries)**
* **2-1-2↓** — Directional down, inside bar, then break below inside bar's low. Mirror of the bullish version.
* **1-2-2↓** — Two consecutive directional down bars after compression.
* **3-1-2↓** — Outside bar followed by compression, then breakdown.

## Full-Time Frame Continuity (FTFC)

This is where TheStrat becomes a professional-grade framework. FTFC means **all timeframes you are analyzing are aligned in the same direction**.

The hierarchy from highest to lowest authority: **Monthly → Weekly → Daily → 4H → 1H → 15m → 5m → Entry TF**

For our 0DTE SPX trading, a simplified version applies:

* **Bias check:** Weekly, Daily, and 4H bar types establish your session direction.
* **Entry check:** 1H and 15m provide the combo structure and trigger.
* **High conviction signal:** All three higher timeframes bullish + 2-1-2↑ on 15m = Sniper entry.
* **Caution signal:** Mixed higher timeframes = reduce size or stand aside.

> When you trade with full timeframe continuity, you are not fighting the market — you are flowing with the dominant order flow. Every opposing player is on the wrong side of every timeframe.

## Why the Spring Analogy Goes Deeper

The Inside Bar is the *point of maximum tension* before resolution. Every trader watching that bar is asking the same question: which way will it break? That collective attention creates the violent move that follows — because the losing side is forced to exit quickly, which adds fuel to the winner's direction.

This is why the 2-1-2 works everywhere, always. Rob Smith didn't invent this behavior. He gave us the clearest framework for seeing and trading it.

## Quick Reference

| Bar Type | Previous Range | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 1 — Inside | Lower high + higher low | Compression, potential energy |
| 2U — Directional Up | New high only | Buyers in control |
| 2D — Directional Down | New low only | Sellers in control |
| 3 — Outside | New high AND new low | Uncertainty, wait for resolution |

**Entry rule:** Always wait for price to break the trigger level. Never enter in anticipation.

**FTFC rule:** Higher timeframes bullish + setup on entry timeframe = high probability. Opposing timeframes = stand aside.
