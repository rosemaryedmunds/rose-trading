# Week 9: The Defense – Rolling & BTC

The best traders in the world are wrong regularly. The difference between professionals and amateurs is not the win rate — it is **what happens when a trade goes against you**.

This week covers the three defensive maneuvers that keep a bad trade from becoming a catastrophic one.

---

## Maneuver 1: The 50% Rule (Managing Winning Trades)

This is the first defense — against your own greed.

When you sell an option for $1.00 and it drops to $0.50 in value, you have captured 50% of the maximum possible profit. The "casino" instinct says "hold for the last $0.50." Experience says "take the money."

**Why the 50% Rule Exists:**

The last 50% of profit takes the same amount of time (or more) to capture as the first 50% — but now you are exposed to market risk with very little remaining reward. The math does not favor holding all the way to expiration.

> **The Rule:** When your option reaches 50% of the premium collected, **Buy to Close (BTC)** and exit the trade. Take the profit. Free up capital. Find the next trade. Do not get greedy.

Applying this rule consistently means you are compounding your returns faster — reinvesting the freed capital into the next premium cycle — rather than waiting weeks for the final few dollars.

---

## Maneuver 2: The Roll (Managing Losing Trades)

If the underlying stock moves against you and threatens your strike price, you do not have to accept defeat. You can "Roll" the position.

Rolling means: buying to close your current position AND simultaneously selling a new position at a better strike price for a later expiration date.

**The Maneuver:**
1. **Buy to Close** your current position (accept the temporary loss at this stage)
2. **Sell to Open** a new position — same strike or moved further away, but for the *next* expiration cycle
3. The new position typically collects more credit than you paid to close — resulting in a **net credit roll**

You are saying: "Okay, you beat me this week. I bet you can't beat me next week." You are buying time using the market's own money.

### When to Roll vs. When to Close Outright

**Roll when:**
* The original thesis is still intact (ribbon still green, chart structure still bullish)
* The move against you looks temporary — noise within a larger uptrend
* You can roll for a net credit (you collect more than you pay)

**Close outright and accept the loss when:**
* The Monthly ribbon has turned red — thesis is broken
* The chart structure has failed — a new lower low has formed
* You have already rolled once and it is still moving against you
* Rolling would require paying a debit — you are compounding a loser

---

## Maneuver 3: The Stop Loss (Broken Thesis)

Sometimes the market is simply telling you that you were wrong. The monthly ribbon flips. The chart breaks. The thesis no longer exists. In these cases, you close the position and accept the loss.

> **The 2x Rule:** If a position reaches a loss of **2× the credit received**, close it immediately. If you sold for $0.50 credit and it is now worth $1.00 — close it. Cut the loss. Protect the account. Live to trade another day.

A 2x loss on a trade where you risked 5% of account (Week 10) is a 10% account drawdown. Painful, but recoverable. Letting a trade go to maximum loss because you "refused to accept it" is a 50%+ drawdown. That can end your trading career.

**The Professional's Mindset on Losses:**

Every loss is the tuition for a lesson. The Sniper who accepts a small, predefined loss and moves on is more dangerous than the stubborn trader who holds losers until they blow up. Small losses protect the capital you need for the next opportunity.

---

## Summary

| Situation | Action | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| Option at 50% of premium | Buy to Close | The 50% Rule |
| Stock approaches your strike (thesis intact) | Roll to next expiration | Roll for net credit |
| Stock approaches your strike (thesis broken) | Close outright | Accept the loss |
| Loss reaches 2× credit received | Close immediately | The 2x Rule |

Mastering defense is what separates consistent Snipers from traders who blow up every six months and start over. Learn these three maneuvers until they are automatic.
