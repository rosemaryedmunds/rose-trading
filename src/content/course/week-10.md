# Week 10: The Shield – Portfolio Allocation

You have a Sniper rifle. That does not mean you fire all your bullets at the first target you see.

The number one reason traders blow up their accounts is not bad analysis. It is **over-leverage** — betting too much on a single trade. This week covers the three shields that protect everything you have built.

---

## Shield 1: The 5% Rule

**Never risk more than 5% of your total account on a single trade.**

| Account Size | Maximum Risk Per Trade |
|---|---|
| $10,000 | $500 |
| $25,000 | $1,250 |
| $50,000 | $2,500 |
| $100,000 | $5,000 |

The 5% rule means a single bad trade — even a maximum loss — removes only 5% of your account. Even five consecutive maximum losses takes you to approximately 23% down (compounded). Your account survives. You continue trading.

Traders who ignore this rule and risk 20–50% per trade are one bad week away from an account-ending event.

**Calculating Your Contract Count:**

If your 5% risk limit is $500 and your spread has a maximum loss of $250 per contract, you can sell **2 contracts** — not 10, not 20. Two. The number of contracts is determined by risk, never by how much premium you want to collect.

> Position sizing is the one variable entirely in your control. Use that control to protect your longevity in the market.

---

## Shield 2: True Diversification

Many traders believe they are diversified when they are not. Holding positions in Apple, Microsoft, and NVIDIA feels diversified — but when the tech sector sells off, all three positions move together. You have not diversified; you have concentrated.

**False Diversification:** AAPL + MSFT + NVDA + AMD. All technology. One sector event = total portfolio damage.

**True Diversification:** One tech trade + one energy trade + one healthcare trade + one consumer staple. Different sectors move independently. One sector's crash does not take everything.

Spread your shots across uncorrelated sectors. Think of it as running multiple Sniper positions in different terrain, watching different targets — if one area goes quiet, the others carry the income.

---

## Shield 3: Dry Powder

Always keep **at least 50% of your account in cash** at all times.

Cash is not idle money. Cash is a **strategic weapon**.

When markets crash — and they will — the trader with cash in hand can deploy at the exact moment when:
* Option premiums are most expensive (high IVR — the ideal time to sell)
* Chart setups are the cleanest (extreme compression after a selloff)
* Everyone else is panicking and getting margin-called

> **The Sniper waits.** The Sniper has ammunition when others are empty. That is when the best trades of the year appear.

The trader who is 100% invested when a selloff hits is forced to watch from the sideline. The trader with 50% cash is rubbing their hands together, opening the option chain, and looking for the next Kill Zone.

---

## Putting the Three Shields Together

A properly protected account at any given time looks like this:

* **Maximum 5% at risk** on any single position
* **Exposure spread** across 3–5 uncorrelated sectors (never clustered)
* **50% of account in cash** — never fully deployed

These three rules, applied consistently, mean that no single event — no matter how dramatic — can take your account out. You are structurally protected from catastrophic loss.

---

## Summary

| Shield | Rule | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| 5% Rule | Never risk more than 5% per trade | Survives losing streaks |
| True Diversification | Spread across uncorrelated sectors | Survives sector crashes |
| Dry Powder | Keep 50%+ in cash | Available for best opportunities |

The best offense is a protected account. You cannot trade when you have no capital. These shields ensure you always have capital.
