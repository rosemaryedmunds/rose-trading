## The Path Forward

### Step 1 — Paper Trade for 4 Weeks
Apply every rule from this curriculum on paper — real charts, real setups, simulated entries and exits. Prove to yourself that you can follow the rules before real money is on the line. Four weeks of consistent paper trading is worth more than months of emotional live trading.

### Step 2 — Fund with a Small Account
Start with an amount you can afford to lose completely. The lessons learned in the first months of live trading are expensive regardless — minimize that tuition. As consistency develops, scale up.

### Step 3 — One Good Trade at a Time
Not a portfolio of 20 simultaneous positions. Not chasing every setup. One high-quality, fully-confluent Kill Zone trade per week is enough to build a track record. Simplicity compounds.

### Step 4 — Keep Your Trade Journal
Every trade recorded. Every decision documented. Review monthly. Identify which setups work, which do not, and which emotional patterns still get in your way. Your journal is the fastest feedback loop available.

### Step 5 — Join the Community
Trading in isolation is harder than it needs to be. Sharing setups, discussing market structure, and learning alongside others accelerates your development faster than any course or book.
