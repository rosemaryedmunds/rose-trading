# Week 7: The Sniper Setup – The Kill Zone

You do not fire at everything that moves. You wait for the target to walk into the crosshairs.

A "Sniper Setup" only exists when **Phase 1 (The Chart) and Phase 2 (The Math) are in perfect agreement simultaneously**. Either element alone is insufficient. A great chart with bad options math is a bad trade. Great math on a broken chart is still a bad trade.

---

## The Kill Zone: Where Chart Meets Math

The Kill Zone is the overlap between technical confirmation and mathematical edge. When both are present at the same time, that is your setup.

### Chart Side (Phase 1 Requirements)
* Monthly/Weekly Ribbon is clearly green (bullish) or red (bearish)
* An Inside Bar or 2-1-2 pattern is visible on your entry timeframe
* TFC is aligned across multiple timeframes in the same direction
* A specific, objective entry trigger level has been identified

### Math Side (Phase 2 Requirements)
* A 30 Delta (or lower) strike is available at acceptable premium
* Theta at that strike provides meaningful daily decay
* IV Rank is above 30 — option premium is elevated, not cheap
* Bid/ask spread is tight — the market is liquid enough to trade

> **The Kill Zone Rule:** ALL items on both sides must be checked before entering. If even one is missing, the trade does not happen. This is the rule that separates disciplined Snipers from impulsive gamblers.

---

## The Confluence Checklist

Before you open your brokerage app, run through these four checkboxes. If even one is missing — **NO TRADE.**

1. **Trend:** Is the Monthly/Weekly chart in your favor? Green Ribbon = Bullish. Red = Bearish. Grey = No trade.
2. **Signal:** Do you have a clear TheStrat signal? Inside Bar break or 2-1-2 combo confirmed?
3. **Math:** Is the option liquid? Is the bid/ask spread tight (under $0.05 for most liquid ETFs)?
4. **Edge:** Can you get 30 Delta or lower AND meaningful Theta at that strike?

All four. Every time. No exceptions.

## The 30-Minute Rule

Amateur traders rush in at 9:30 AM when the opening bell rings. They fight the algorithms over the opening price. They get whipsawed and wonder why they can't win.

Snipers wait.

The first 30 minutes of the trading day are a price discovery battle between institutional algorithms. The real direction of the day often does not establish itself until after 10:00 AM EST.

> **Entry Rule:** We do not enter new positions before **10:00 AM EST**. We watch, we evaluate, we wait for the dust to settle and the first clean setup to emerge. Patience is the edge.

---

## Implied Volatility Rank (IVR)

IV Rank measures current Implied Volatility relative to the past year's range.

* **IVR 0** = IV at its annual low. Options are cheap. Do not sell cheap things.
* **IVR 100** = IV at its annual high. Options are very expensive. Ideal time to sell.

As option sellers, we want to sell when premium is *expensive*. High IVR means fear is elevated — buyers are overpaying for protection. We sell them overpriced protection and collect the excess premium.

| IVR Range | Condition | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 0–20 | Cheap IV | Avoid — not worth selling |
| 20–30 | Acceptable IV | Proceed with caution |
| 30–60 | Ideal zone | Sell premium — this is your target |
| 60–100 | Elevated IV | Best premiums — strong sell opportunity |

## Sizing the Target

Never full-size a position just because the setup looks great. Position sizing is determined by risk (Week 10), not by how much premium you want to collect.

Once the chart and math both confirm a Kill Zone entry, the correct next step is:

1. Determine your maximum dollar risk for the trade (5% rule — covered in Week 10)
2. Find the strike that hits 30 Delta with high IVR
3. Calculate the number of contracts that keeps risk within your limit
4. Enter with a limit order (never market orders — covered in Week 8)

## Summary

The Sniper Setup is not a gut feeling. It is a checklist. When every box is checked — chart aligned, signal confirmed, math favorable, premium elevated — you have a high-probability trade.

When any box is empty, you do not have a trade. You have a speculation. And we are not speculators.
